Genesis 1:1

On the origin of websites.

Date, Time

In the beginning Tom created the repo. Now the project was fileless and empty, darkness was over the surface of VSCode, and the cursor of Tom was hovering over console.
Tom said, "ng new my-portfolio" and there was an awful lot of files. Tom saw that the file explorer was massive, and he separated the project from the chaos. Tom called the project “index.html,” and the chaos he called "npm uninstall angular". And there was simplicity, and there was peace—the first site.

As much as I find myself enjoying being The Almighty in my own digital kingdom, for the sake of my own sanity I'll leave the more traditional allegoric prose behind.
This is my website. It isn't much, but it's mine. Whether it will eventually resemble the promised land I'm envisaging, or collapse into some kind of elysian fields for my heroic but ultimately doomed attempts to write code, only time will tell. But optimism is free, and I'm a right sucker for a bargain.
